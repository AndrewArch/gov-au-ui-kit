Our role

Through our analysis, advice and programs we aim to make the arts and communications sectors better for everyone.

Responsibilities

We help the communications sector stay competitive and innovative by providing advice, delivering programs and conducting research.

We also manage programs that encourage artists, support Australia's cultural heritage and help the public to experience arts and culture.

Information and services

Internet

Providing access to high-quality, reliable and affordable internet services.

  • Internet speeds
  • Internet safety

Television

Regulating the TV industry and its services.

  • Television reception
  • Television content rules

Phone

Ensuring everyone has reasonable access to reliable phone services.

  • Mobile phone black spots
  • Accessible phone services

Radio

Giving everyone access to radio and assisting in the roll-out of digital radio.

  • Digital radio
  • Content classification

Post

Advising the Australian Government on how the postal industry can best serve the public.

  • Posting in Australia and overseas

Spectrum

Ensuring the electromagnetic spectrum can be used with current electronic devices.

  • Spectrum licences
  • Emergency services
  • Digital dividend spectrum

Copyright

Providing legal protection for people who express ideas and information in certain forms.

  • Copyright
  • Intellectual property (IP)

Classification

Helping the Classification Board to make decisions about the classification of films, television, books and music.

  • The classification code

Arts

Encouraging excellence in art, support for cultural heritage and public access to arts and culture.

  • Prime Minister's Literary Awards
  • Lending rights
  • Arts funding

Portfolio agencies we work with

We work closely with other Government agencies within the Communications portfolio to provide all Australians with information and services.

A portolio is an area of responsibility for how Australia is run. The Communications portfolio is led by the Minister for Communications.

Australia Council for the Arts

The Australian Government's arts funding and advisory body.

Australia Post

Providing reliable and affordable postal, retail, financial and travel services.

Australian Broadcasting corporation (ABC)

Australia’s public broadcaster, including national and local television and radio programs.

Australian Communications and Media Authority (ACMA)

Responsible for the regulation of broadcasting, the internet, radiocommunications and telecommunications.

Australian Film Television and Radio School (AFTRS)

The leading institution for the education and training of creative talent.

Australian National Maritime Museum

Australia’s national centre for maritime collections, exhibitions, research and archaeology.

Bundanon Trust

Supporting arts practice and engagement through education, exhibition and performance programs.

Australia Business Arts Foundation (operating as Creative Partnerships Australia)

Bringing artists, donors, businesses and arts organisations together to promote a more sustainable and vibrant arts sector.

The Museum of Australian Democracy

Helping people to understand Australia’s social and political history by interpreting the past and present and exploring the future.

National Film and Sound Archive

The nation’s living archive – collecting, preserving and sharing our rich audiovisual heritage.

National Gallery of Australia (NGA)

Australia's national cultural institution for the visual arts.

National Library of Australia (NLA)

Ensuring that important documentary resources are collected, preserved and made accessible to the public.

National Museum of Australia

A social history museum exploring the land, nation and people of Australia.

National Portrait Gallery Australia (NPGA)

The national collection of portraits of Australians reflects the breadth and energy of Australian culture and endeavour.

NBN Co Limited

Delivering Australia’s first national, open access broadband network to all Australians.

Office of the Children’s e-Safety Commissioner

Committed to helping young people have safe, positive experiences online.

Screen Australia

Funding the production of everything from feature films to documentaries, television drama, children’s programs and online web series.

Special broadcasting service (SBS)

Australia’s multicultural and multilingual broadcaster.
